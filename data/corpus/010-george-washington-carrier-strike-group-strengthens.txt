George Washington Carrier Strike Group strengthens relationships with Australia

By Mass Communication Specialist 3rd Class Brian Sloan

CORAL SEA (June 18, 2015) – The Nimitz-class aircraft carrier USS George Washington (CVN 73) hosted distinguished visitors from Australia to receive a first-hand look into aircraft carrier at-sea operations, June 18.

The visit allowed George Washington’s Australian partners to observe aircraft launch and recovery and learn about aircraft carrier operations.

Ambassador John Berry, U.S. ambassador to Australia, and Stuart Robert, Australia’s assistant minister for defense, were among the 18 guests welcomed on board.

“We’re extremely honored to be aboard the ship and experience such great hospitality from the admiral and his crew,” said Berry. “It’s such a pleasure to see firsthand all the high-tempo work that George Washington’s crew performs.”

According to Rear Adm. John Alexander, commander, Battle Force 7th Fleet, the opportunity to bring high-level Australian government and military officials aboard George Washington shows the strength of the U.S. and Australian alliance.

“This embark brings new levels of understanding that help foster and build the relationship between our two great nations,” said Alexander. “Today’s visit by the Ambassador was an official welcome by senior members of the Australian government for the George Washington Carrier Strike Group to Brisbane.” 

George Washington and its embarked air wing, CVW 5, are on patrol in the 7th Fleet area of responsibility supporting security and stability in the Indo-Asia-Pacific region. George Washington will conduct a hull-swap with Nimitz-class aircraft carrier USS Ronald Reagan (CVN 76) later this year after serving seven years as the U.S. Navy's only forward-deployed aircraft carrier in Yokosuka, Japan.
